Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic, metabolic disease characterized by elevated levels of blood glucose (or blood sugar), which leads over time to serious damage to the heart, blood vessels, eyes, kidneys and nerves.

Do's
  • Monitor your blood sugar levels regularly and keep a record of them.
  • Follow a balanced diet, including carbohydrates, proteins, and healthy fats.
  • Exercise regularly as recommended by your doctor.
  • Take diabetes medications or insulin as prescribed by your doctor.
  • Visit your doctor for regular check-ups and screenings.
  • Wear medical identification jewelry or carry a diabetes identification card in case of a medical emergency.
  • Learn to recognize the signs of high or low blood sugar and take actions accordingly.
  • Practice good foot care and inspect your feet for any signs of injury or infection regularly.
  • Keep your stress levels under control.
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water and avoiding sugary drinks.
Dont's
  • Do not skip meals or delay meals.
  • Do not overeat or consume too many sugary or high-carbohydrate foods.
  • Do not smoke or consume alcohol excessively.
  • Do not ignore your healthcare providers' instructions or stop taking your medications without their approval.
  • Do not ignore symptoms of high or low blood sugar and take appropriate action.
  • Do not expose your feet to extreme temperatures or wear ill-fitting shoes.
  • Do not neglect your dental health.
  • Do not use any medication or supplement without consulting with your healthcare provider.
  • Do not ignore any signs of depression or anxiety.
  • Do not forget to communicate with your family and friends about your struggles and challenges with diabetes.

Mouth Ulcer

A mouth ulcer, also known as canker sores or aphthous ulcer, is a painful sore that develops inside the mouth. It can occur on the tongue, gums, inside of the cheeks, or on the roof of the mouth.

Mouth ulcers usually heal on their own within a week or two. However, if they persist for more than a few weeks or are accompanied by other symptoms, it may be a sign of an underlying medical condition, and medical attention should be sought. Over-the-counter medications, such as antiseptic mouthwash and pain relievers can help reduce symptoms. Severe cases may require prescription medication.

Do's
  • Keep the area around the ulcer clean by brushing and flossing regularly.
  • Use a soft-bristled toothbrush to avoid irritating the ulcer.
  • Rinse your mouth with warm salt water or mouthwash to reduce irritation and inflammation.
Dont's
  • Avoid spicy, acidic, or salty foods which can irritate the ulcer.
  • Do not drink alcohol or smoke as they can cause additional irritation.
  • Do not touch the ulcer with your hands or tongue as this can spread the infection

Insomnia

Insomnia is difficulty getting to sleep or staying asleep for long enough to feel refreshed the next morning. It’s a common problem thought to regularly affect around one in every three people and is particularly common in elderly people.

Hypertension

Hypertension, also known as high blood pressure, is a chronic medical condition in which the force of blood against the walls of the arteries is consistently elevated above normal levels. Hypertension is a major risk factor for heart disease, stroke, and other health problems.

Do's
  • Monitor your blood pressure regularly at home or at your doctor's office.
  • Take your blood pressure medication as prescribed by your doctor.
  • Maintain a healthy weight by following a balanced diet and engaging in regular physical activity.
  • Reduce your salt intake to less than 2,300 milligrams per day.
  • Manage your stress levels through relaxation techniques such as deep breathing exercises, yoga, or meditation.
  • Quit smoking, as it increases your blood pressure and your risk of heart disease.
Dont's
  • Do not skip your medication or change the dosage without consulting your doctor.
  • Avoid foods that are high in saturated and trans fats, such as fried foods, processed meats, and baked goods.
  • Do not consume excessive amounts of caffeine, which can raise your blood pressure.
  • Do not engage in activities that can cause sudden increases in blood pressure, such as weightlifting or heavy lifting.
  • Do not take over-the-counter medications, such as decongestants, without first consulting your doctor, as they can increase blood pressure.
  • Do not ignore your blood pressure readings, as uncontrolled hypertension can lead to serious health complications.
